Cattle, Displacement and Counterinsurgency in Guatemala:

How is beef production linked to landscape change and social conflict?

Using the ArcGis Story Maps tool, I created a series of visualizations about how beef production is linked to landscape change and social conflict in Central America. This project is an example of ‘Scrollytelling’ – a long-form infographic with a narrative arc. I based the argument on a book chapter by Robert G. Williams about the Central American cattle boom of the 1960s and 1970s, in Export Agriculture and the Crisis in Central America (1986). This story map uses pictorial graphics, a bar chart, a bivariate map, a historical map, text and images to advance the narrative. View my story map here.
