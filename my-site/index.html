Anika Rice

I'm a graduate student in Geography at the University of Wisconsin Madison. I study agroecology and land-based social movements through a feminsit political ecology lens. I currently work with agroecological farming organizations across Guatemala, evaluating adaptation strategies and rural transformations during crises such as COVID-19, as well as asking questions about solidarity and gendered access to resources. As a geographer, I think spatially about human-environment interactions, intersectionality and difference. You can find my research, writing and cartographic work here.
